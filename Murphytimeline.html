Historical Timeline



Trevithick Invents Locomotive

1804

Richard Trevithick’s steam locomotive runs on an industrial rail-line in Britain, marking an early milestone in the Industrial Revolution, enhancing transportation.

Fulton Builds Steamboat

1807

Robert Fulton builds the Clermont, the first paddle-wheel steamboat, improving transportation across U.S. waterways, aiding industrialization.

Congress of Vienna Meets

1814

Congress of Vienna begins to restore the old order after Napoleon’s defeat, as European powers redraw the map to maintain a balance of power.

Shelley Writes Frankenstein

1816

Mary Shelley writes Frankenstein, a key romantic work reflecting fears of industrialization and scientific advances.

Mexico Gains Independence

1821

Mexico declares independence from Spain, part of a wave of nationalist movements in the Americas.

Liberals Overthrow Charles X

1830

Liberals overthrow Charles X in France, establishing a constitutional monarchy, driven by liberalism and nationalism.

Great Reform Act Passed

1832

Great Reform Act in Britain expands voting rights to the industrial middle class, strengthening liberalism and stabilizing Britain politically.

Opium War Starts

1839

Opium War begins in China, signaling Western imperial pressure on East Asia.

Revolutions Sweep Europe

1848

Revolutions erupt across Europe, starting with the overthrow of Louis-Philippe in France, as liberals and nationalists challenge conservative regimes.

Great Exhibition Opens

1851

Great Exhibition opens in London’s Crystal Palace, showcasing industrial progress and Britain’s global dominance.

Louis-Napoleon Becomes Emperor

1852

Louis-Napoleon becomes Napoleon III, establishing the Second Empire in France, marking a return to authoritarian rule.

Treaty of Paris Signed

1856

Treaty of Paris ends the Crimean War, weakening the Concert of Europe and opening the door to Italian and German unification.

Darwin Publishes Origin

1859

Charles Darwin publishes On the Origin of Species, introducing natural selection and challenging traditional views of humanity.

Garibaldi Conquers Sicily

1860

Giuseppe Garibaldi conquers Sicily and Naples for Italian unification, a key step toward a unified Italy under Victor Emmanuel II.

Alexander II Frees Serfs

1861

Czar Alexander II emancipates Russian serfs, attempting reform but failing to fully modernize Russia.

Civil War Begins

1861

American Civil War begins, a struggle over slavery and national unity.

Prussia Defeats Austria

1866

Prussia defeats Austria in the Austro-Prussian War, gaining Venetia for Italy, advancing German and Italian unification.

Suez Canal Completed

1869

Suez Canal completed, enhancing global trade and Western imperialism.

Franco-Prussian War Starts

1870

Franco-Prussian War begins, leading to German unification as Bismarck uses war to unite Germany under William I.

William I Becomes Kaiser

1871

William I proclaimed Kaiser of the Second German Empire, completing German unification at Versailles.

Bell Invents Telephone

1876

Alexander Graham Bell invents the telephone, revolutionizing communication during the Second Industrial Revolution.

Alexander II Assassinated

1881

Alexander II assassinated; Alexander III turns to repression, ending reform efforts in Russia.

Dreyfus Affair Begins

1894

Dreyfus Affair begins in France, fueling anti-Semitism, highlighting social tensions and inspiring Zionism.

Marconi Sends Radio Waves

1901

Guglielmo Marconi sends the first radio waves across the Atlantic, advancing global communication technology.

Bloody Sunday Sparks Unrest

1905

Bloody Sunday in St. Petersburg sparks Russian unrest, leading to the creation of the Duma under Nicholas II.
